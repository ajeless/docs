# Principles

These principles are not implementation decisions. They are guardrails for ideation, research, and eventual design.

## 1. Open source and community-driven

The platform should be meaningfully open to inspection, extension, and deep modification. Community participation should not be limited to asset creation or scripting around a closed core.

## 2. Modding-first

Modding is not an afterthought. The platform should eventually provide first-class experiences for multiple kinds of creators, from lightweight tinkerers to deep forkers.

## 3. Preserve a layered creator experience

Different creators need different depths of access.

At minimum, the platform should support:

- local/low-friction modification
- systems-level composition
- substrate-level alteration

A clean separation between those roles should not become a caste system, but it should inform tooling and expectations.

## 4. Avoid premature commitment

This repo should preserve ideas, hypotheses, and research directions without prematurely converting them into architecture. Attractive concepts should be treated as working models unless and until they survive pressure testing.

## 5. Prefer explicit boundaries over hype

The project should clearly distinguish between:

- software-enforceable systems
- author-defined mechanics
- emergent social or economic behavior

This is essential for staying grounded and avoiding vaporware.

## 6. Scale should not require a different conceptual model

A tiny room and a massive world may demand different performance strategies, but ideally they should not require completely different ways of thinking about authored reality.

## 7. Realism should be configurable

The platform should not assume that all worthwhile realities are highly realistic. Authors should be able to tune experiences toward arcade, tactical, cinematic, surreal, or otherwise custom regimes.

## 8. Persistence should be configurable

Not every experience needs a forever-world. Persistence should eventually be treated as a configurable policy or profile, not a universal requirement.

## 9. Keep the door open to both direct and abstract simulation

Some things should be simulated directly. Other things should be abstracted, reconstructed, approximated, or delegated. The design should remain open to variable fidelity rather than demanding full detail everywhere.

## 10. Keep the human experience in view

Even when discussing kernels, layers, coordinates, and simulation, the platform exists to enable meaningful creation and play. Technical architecture should remain accountable to author experience and player experience.

## 11. Separate ideas from decisions in the repo

The repo should make it obvious what is:

- a vision statement
- a working mental model
- a speculative idea
- a research topic
- an open question
- an actual decision

That clarity will help both humans and coding agents collaborate productively.
