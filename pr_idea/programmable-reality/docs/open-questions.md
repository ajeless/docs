# Open Questions

This document tracks the most important unresolved questions.

The goal is not to eliminate uncertainty quickly. The goal is to keep uncertainty visible, organized, and productive.

## 1. Vocabulary and ontology

- What exactly counts as a programmable “law”?
- What is the difference between entity, actor, observer, world, system, event, and scope?
- What terms are metaphors, and what terms should survive into real design language?

## 2. Reality boundaries

- What belongs in hard reality versus institutional mechanics versus emergent reality?
- Which categories are actually core substrate concerns?
- Which should be treated as authored rules rather than kernel concerns?

## 3. Kernel scope

- What belongs in the Reality Kernel?
- Is “kernel” even the right metaphor?
- Which services are true foundations, and which are cross-cutting orchestration concerns?

## 4. Spatial model

- How should the platform think about locality, scale, and coordinate representation?
- What mental model best bridges small local play and very large worlds?
- How much of the “Spatial OS” framing is useful model versus premature architecture?

## 5. Information model

- Should information propagation be a configurable law-like property?
- What should authors be able to configure about observability, sensing, delay, and hidden state?
- How should the platform distinguish physical signal rules from ordinary multiplayer synchronization?

## 6. Persistence model

- Should persistence be off/on, a slider, a set of profiles, or layered policy?
- What kinds of world memory matter most?
- What should be saved directly, reconstructed later, or discarded?

## 7. Simulation model

- What is simulated directly?
- What is abstracted?
- What is approximated?
- What is delegated to AI-assisted orchestration?
- How should the platform describe variable fidelity in a creator-friendly way?

## 8. AI role

- Should AI be conceptualized mainly as tooling, orchestration, world participation, or all three?
- Where does AI make the platform more powerful?
- Where does AI risk making the model less clear or less controllable?

## 9. Multiplayer and shared reality

- What should shared reality mean across scale?
- What does it mean for multiple observers to inhabit worlds with different scopes or levels of simulation?
- How should the platform support both local/private experiences and larger persistent shared environments?

## 10. Modding model

- What is the right creator experience for each author type?
- How should safe customization differ from systems authoring?
- How should deep forking remain possible without making everything else fragile?

## 11. Minimum viable form

- What is the smallest prototype that meaningfully tests the core vision?
- Which research fronts need proof-of-concept work first?
- What can be deferred without losing the essence of the idea?

## 12. Naming and identity

- Is “Programmable Reality” the best umbrella term?
- Is “Reality Kernel” or “Reality Engine” the right project name?
- Which names are useful as metaphors but risky as public-facing identity?
